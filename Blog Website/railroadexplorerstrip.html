Railroad Explorers California

November 2025 | Ione, California

An Overview

Have you ever wanted to experience something you always thought about as a child? Well, that was the case for me, as a child, an interesting thought kept popping up into my head: " How would it feel like to be THE train instead of a passenger on the train?" It sounds weird, yes, but I was a kid, and as train enthusiast (ask me what a "Dash 8" locomotive is, and I will give you an unnecesarily long breakdown) this place really made my younger self happy. Enough with the childhood wonders of mine, and let me give you an overview of this place. Railroad Explorers is located nearby a mine in Ione, California, that was dug out during the Gold Rush in the 1800s, it has been preserved, and is open to the public currently. If you ask me what it is exactly, I would say think of a Minecraft Mineshaft but it is not underground, but at the same time if you do not play Minecraft I got you. The place consists of an old railroad with "train cycles" I like to call them, they are basically minecarts with bicycle like pedals and features on them. You can ride these cycles on the railroad tracks that go up a hill and into an interesting area where you can see the remains of the mine itself. The cycles are powered by a motor, which needs minimal pedalling for it to activate and get you going, but you can also pedal to go faster if you want. The ride is super fun, and the views are pleasant, as you can see the whole area from the top of the hill, and you can also see the cattle that is on the property. The ride itself is about 1 hour to and from the top of the hill, and it is a very fun experience. Overall, I had a great time at Railroad Explorers, and I would highly recommend it to anyone looking for a unique and fun experience in California. I will go in detail down below, and give you a breakdown of the whole experience, advice, and the area's history because it is fascinating.

What the Experience Was Like and What to Expect

Hidden in the hills near Lodi/Sacramento area, lies this unique place. My family and I went with one of our friends, so this information may help some people who are traveling as a group quite a bit.
- First things first, if you are going during the winter, PLEASE bring a heavy jacket, gloves, and a beanie. It gets very cold during the ride, and on top of the hill.
- The crew members are extremely nice to everyone, and they are very helpful, so do not hesitate to ask them any questions you have about the place, the history, or the ride itself. They will be more than happy to help you out.
- The ride is about 45 minutes to an hour long to the mountain, and coming back down.
- The ride does grant you access to view the wildlife on top of the hill, which include cattles, and some other animals.
- What I meant by "access" is that you can see the animals from the train, but you cannot get off the train and go pet them, so do not get your hopes up.
- Make sure to hold on to your phones, or any sort of camera you bring (I highly recommend bringing a 360 camera if you have one) because the car is going on rails, so it can be a bit bumpy.
- The mechanism they use to flip the carts at the top of the hill is very interesting, and I won't say much about it, because I want you to see it for yourself (or search it on youtube).
- There is a fireplace conveniently placed at the start of the ride, so you can warm up before you start the ride, and after you come back down. It is a nice touch, and it is very useful during the winter.
- The ride is very fun, and it is a unique experience that you cannot find anywhere else. It is definitely worth the trip if you are in the area, and it is a great way to spend a day with friends or family.
